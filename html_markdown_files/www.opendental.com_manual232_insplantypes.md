<div class="TopBar2"><p>Insurance Plan Types</p></div>
<div class="GeneralPageContent">
<p>Different types of <a href="insplan.html">Insurance Plans</a> require different settings. When entering insurance plan information, there are four different plan type options and several areas that affect fee schedules.</p>
<p>The links below explain which plan types to use for different insurance plan scenarios and how to set fee schedules and other settings.</p>
<p>For additional help determining what plan type is best for your scenario see: <a href="https://opendental.com/resources/Insurance%20Flow%20Chart.pdf">Insurance Flow Chart</a>.</p>
<p class="MarginBottomZero"></p>
<ul class="MarginBottomGap">
<li><a href="planppo.html">PPO Insurance Plan</a>: For use with in-network plans pays at a percentage. Can track write-offs.</li>
<li><a href="planppofixed.html">PPO Fixed Benefit Insurance Plan</a>: For use with in-network plans where insurance pays at fixed amount. Tracks write-offs.</li>
<li><a href="plancatperc.html">Category Percentage Insurance Plan</a>: Generally for out-of-network plans. Can be used with or without fee schedules or <a href="bluebooksetup.html">Blue Book</a>. Does not track write-offs.</li>
<li><a href="planhmosupplemental.html">HMO / DMO Insurance Plan with Co-pays</a> (e.g. Texas plans): Track write-offs.</li>
<li><a href="planmedicaid.html">Medicaid or Flat Co-Pay Insurance Plan</a>: 100% coverage (no percentages, maximums or deductibles). Does not track write-offs.</li>
<li><a href="plancapitation.html">Capitation ( HMO / DMO ) Insurance Plan</a>: Office receives a flat fee every month. Patients may pay a flat fee for some procedures and no fee for others.</li>
</ul>
