<h2>Text Messaging</h2>
<p>It is the responsibility of the office to obtain consent to send a patient text messages. </p>
<p class="MarginBottomZero">Patients can give consent when they manually provide their mobile phone number. This can happen when: </p>
<ul class="MarginBottomGap">
<li>Creating an appointment via <a href="webschednewpatsetup.html">Web Sched New Patient</a>.</li>
<li>Filling <a href="patientform.html">Patient Forms</a>.</li>
<li>Filling <a href="../site/webforms.html">Web Forms</a>.</li>
<li>Filling forms via <a href="../site/eclipboard.html">eClipboard</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Alternatively, the office can verbally obtain consent. Once received, record the Text OK status in the <a href="patientedit.html">Edit Patient Information</a> window.</p>
<p>In order to reduce outgoing SMS rejection rates, Open Dental attempts to shorten outgoing URLs with <i>patientviewer</i> branded sites. If unbranded URLs are not caught by Open Dental and are continually sent to phone carriers, your text messaging service may be cancelled by the carrier without notice.</p>
<h2>Patient Responses</h2>
<p><b>HELP</b><br/> If Open Dental HQ detects a message with a response of HELP, we send back a message with a help link. </p>
<p><b>START / OPTIN</b><br/> If a patient has opted out of text messaging, they can opt back in anytime by texting either START or OPTIN to the office. </p>
<p><b>STOP / END / QUIT / UNSUBSCRIBE</b><br/> Patients can opt out of messages anytime. When a patient responds to a message with one of the following: STOP, END, QUIT, or UNSUBSCRIBE, a message is sent back informing the patient they have been opted out and will receive no further messages. This message is also forwarded to the office. </p>
<p>Open Dental HQ has a record of the patient opting out. At the office, the Text OK status for the patient is set to No with a commlog indicating the opt out request. While the office may attempt to continue sending a message, all messages to patients who have opted out are blocked by Open Dental HQ. A patient must text either START or OPTIN to allow receiving messages again.</p>
