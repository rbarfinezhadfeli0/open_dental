			<h1>EHR Attestation</h1>
		</div>
		<div id="Content">
<p>To receive an <a href="ehrlicense.html">EHR/PI</a> incentive payment each year, you need to go through attestation with your state Medicaid office or CMS (Medicare). See <a href="https://www.cms.gov/Regulations-and-Guidance/Legislation/EHRIncentivePrograms/RegistrationandAttestation.html">Registration and Attestation</a> on the cms.gov website.</p>
<p>How to avoid mistakes that could cost you money:</p>
<ol>
<li>Count on being audited. Retain supporting documentation for at least 6 years. The audit rate for EHR is high. See <a href="ehraudits.html">EHR Audits</a>.</li>
<li>Enter into an EHR contract with Open Dental. Do not apply retroactively for an EHR incentive participation year if you did not have an EHR contract for that year.</li>
<li>Certification for EHR is a vendor/product combination, not just a product. CMS dictates that any contract for an EHR purchase must be directly with the vendor. Thus, EHR-certified versions of Open Dental can only be purchased directly from Open Dental. You cannot purchase an EHR-certified version of Open Dental from a reseller.</li>
<li>For AIU, make sure your AIU participation year matches the EHR contract year.  <div class="Note">Note:  2016 was the last year to start participation in the EHR Medicaid Incentive Program (AIU).</div>
</li>
<li>If you have accepted an incentive payment, do not cancel your Open Dental support or contract early.</li>
<li>Make sure you meet EHR incentive program requirements before accepting payment.</li>
<li>Don't make up numbers when reporting meaningful use. If you are using Open Dental EHR as designed, your reporting will provide the accurate figures.</li>
<li>You must comply with HIPAA requirements and have a Security Risk Analysis (SRA) in place. Do not claim you had an SRA if you did not.</li>
</ol>
<p>Providers who make a mistake during attestation for AIU can self report and have their year changed.</p>
