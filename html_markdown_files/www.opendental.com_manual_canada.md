<h2>Software As a Medical Device (SaMD)</h2>
<p> Per Health Canada, Open Dental Software does not need to be classified as a Medical Device as we meet the exclusion criteria below.</p>
<p>The following information can be found in detail here: <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/public-involvement-consultations/medical-devices/software-medical-device-draft-guidance/document.html#a22" target="_blank">Draft Guidance Document - Software as a Medical Device (SaMD)</a><br/><i>2.2 Exclusion criteria<br/> The medical purposes described in the device definition of the Act can apply to a wide range of products. However, software that does not have a direct impact on the diagnosis, treatment, or management of an individual's disease, disorder, abnormal physical state or symptoms would not be subject to the Regulations (e.g. a mobile app intended to monitor daily calorie intake and energy expenditure to allow an individual to self-manage their weight).<br/><br/> It has been Health Canada's longstanding position that the following types of software do not meet the definition of a medical device and are therefore not subject to the Regulations: <ul>
<li>Software intended for administrative support of a healthcare facility,</li>
<li>Software that enables clinical communication and workflow including patient registration, scheduling visits, voice calling, video calling,</li>
<li>Software intended for maintaining or encouraging a healthy lifestyle, such as general wellness apps, and</li>
<li>Software intended to serve as electronic patient records.</li>
</ul>
</i></p>
<p>Additional clarifying information can be found here: <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/medical-devices/activities/announcements/questions-answers-software-regulated-medical-device.html" target="_blank">Software Regulated as a Medical Device - Frequently Asked Questions</a></p>
